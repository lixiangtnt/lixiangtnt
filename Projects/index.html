  2. Carbon fibre recycling

    Recovering value from carbon fibre reinforced polymers waste can help to address the high cost and environmental burden of producing carbon fibres, but there is limited understanding of the cost and environmental implications of potential recycling technologies. The objective of this study is to assess the environmental and financial viability of mechanical recycling of carbon fibre composite waste.

  3. Low Carbon Manufacturing for Automotive Components

    Participate in a RMB 19m project funded by Ningbo Science and Technology Bureau (9m) and the University of Nottingham Ningbo China (10m).
    The subproject I worked on that focuses on life cycle assessment based optimisation for automotive engineering. I co-developed a life cycle assessment platform for automotive components (a Web-based software and a Theoretical model), evaluated via an automotive component case study through collaboration with an industrial partner. I established an analysis framework of carbon fibre recycling on both environmental and economic aspects. I developed a novel multi-objective optimisation model by integrating life cycle assessment and life cycle cost.

  4. Calibration of Optical 3D Scanner using 6 DoF Robot

    Developed Matlab code to drive Hexapod (6 DoF robot) and to initial 3-D scan. Designed a fixture (Using N.X 7.5) to allow the calibration artefact to be placed in three orthogonal positions on the Hexapod. Performed verification experiments and undertake analysis to compute length measurement error distributions based on Hough Transform Algorithm. 5.

  5. Lawn Automatic Water Filling & Monitoring System

    Served as Project leader, funded by CEPF
    Led a team to develop an automatic system to monitoring the environmental parameters based on a designed intelligent auto. This project has been reported by many Chinese Presses (e.g. China Daily, ifeng, NBTV) as well as University of Nottingham website.
